# Acme Corp Sales Process

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

- **Company size**: 50 to 500 engineers
- **Industry**: SaaS, fintech, developer tools
- **Pain point**: Fragmented developer workflow across 5 or more tools
- **Budget**: $50k to $200k ARR
- **Champion**: VP Engineering or CTO
- **Disqualifiers**: Fewer than 20 engineers (too small for ROI), government contracts requiring FedRAMP (not yet certified), companies already locked into multi-year competitor contracts

## Sales Process Stages

| Stage | Actions | Exit Criteria | Typical Duration |
|-------|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| Prospecting | Identify ICP matches, warm intros via LinkedIn, respond to inbound leads | Meeting booked | 1-5 days |
| Discovery | 30-min call, understand tech stack and pain points | BANT qualified (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) | 1 call |
| Demo | Live product demo tailored to their stack, invite technical stakeholders | Technical validation confirmed | 1-2 sessions |
| Proposal | Pricing and ROI model, legal review, security questionnaire | Verbal agreement from decision maker | 5-10 days |
| Close | Contract signing, onboarding handoff to customer success | Signed deal in CRM | 3-5 days |

Average cycle: 45 days (enterprise), 21 days (mid-market).

## Pricing

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| Team | $15/user/month | Core features, 5 integrations, community support |
| Business | $35/user/month | Advanced search, SSO, priority support, unlimited integrations |
| Enterprise | Custom | Dedicated instance, SLA, custom integrations, onboarding support |

All plans are annual billing. Monthly billing available at 20% premium. Volume discounts: 10% for 100+ seats, 15% for 250+ seats.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Competitor A (ToolFlex)**: Strong in CI/CD but weak on search. We win on unified developer experience and breadth of integrations. They are 30% cheaper on their base tier but lack SSO and enterprise features.
- **Competitor B (DevOpen)**: Open-source core, paid hosting. We win on enterprise features, support responsiveness, and compliance certifications. Their free tier attracts smaller teams but conversion to paid is low.
- **Competitor C (CodeAI)**: AI-first approach with code generation focus. We win on reliability, breadth of integrations, and production-readiness. Their AI features are impressive but generate false positives that frustrate senior engineers.

## Objection Handling

- "We already use Competitor A" — Focus on integration breadth, show switching cost ROI calculator. Average switching payback: 4 months. Offer 60-day trial with migration support.
- "Too expensive" — Quantify developer time savings: $150k or more per 100 engineers annually based on customer survey data. Offer quarterly payment plan for budget-constrained teams.
- "Security concerns" — Point to SOC 2 Type II (audit in progress, expected completion May 2026), data residency options (US and EU), and encryption at rest and in transit.
- "We need to evaluate more options" — Offer a 2-week proof of concept with a dedicated solutions engineer. 78% of PoC participants convert to paid.
- "Not the right time" — Schedule a 6-month follow-up and add to nurture sequence. Share relevant case study in the interim.

## Commission Structure

Base salary plus commission. Commission: 10% of first-year ACV for new logos, 5% for expansion. Accelerators kick in at 110% of quota: 15% for new and 8% for expansion. Quota is set quarterly. Pipeline target: 3x quota coverage. Commission is paid monthly, 30 days after deal close.
