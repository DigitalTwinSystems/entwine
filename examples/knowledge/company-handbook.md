# Acme Corp Employee Handbook

## Mission

Acme Corp builds developer tooling that makes engineering teams more productive. We believe great tools should be invisible — they remove friction so developers can focus on what matters. Founded in 2021, Acme Corp now serves over 400 customers across SaaS, fintech, and developer tools industries.

## Values

- **Ship fast, iterate faster** — We prefer small, frequent releases over big-bang launches. Every feature ships behind a flag. Our target is to deploy to production at least twice daily. If a release causes issues, we roll back within 5 minutes and investigate — blame is never assigned to individuals.
- **Data over opinions** — Decisions are backed by metrics. When data is ambiguous, we run experiments. Every product feature has a hypothesis and measurable success criteria defined before development begins.
- **Radical transparency** — Engineering decisions, roadmap priorities, and financial performance are shared company-wide. Our monthly all-hands includes a full financial review so every employee understands how the business is performing.
- **Own your domain** — Every team member is empowered to make decisions within their area. Escalate only when cross-team alignment is needed. We trust people to make good decisions and learn from mistakes.

## Working Hours & Communication

- Core hours: 10:00–15:00 local time (overlap window for sync meetings). Outside core hours, async communication is expected.
- Default to async: Slack messages, GitHub issues, and email over meetings. If a topic requires more than 3 back-and-forth messages, schedule a quick call.
- Daily standup: 09:00 UTC, 15 minutes max, CEO-led. Format: what I did, what I am doing, any blockers.
- All-hands: First Monday of each month, 30 minutes. Covers company metrics, product updates, and open Q&A.
- Team retrospectives: End of each sprint (bi-weekly). Led by the team lead, results shared in #retros.

## PTO Policy

Unlimited PTO with a 2-week minimum per year. Manager approval required for absences longer than 5 consecutive business days. No PTO during code freezes (announced 2 weeks in advance). We encourage employees to take at least one full week off per quarter to recharge. PTO does not accrue or carry over — there is no payout upon departure.

## Equipment & Stipends

All employees receive a MacBook Pro (M-series), external monitor, and $500 home-office stipend upon joining. Replacement cycle: 3 years. Additional equipment requests are handled by the IT team via the #it-support Slack channel. Employees who attend conferences receive a $1,500 annual conference budget covering registration, travel, and accommodation.

## Benefits

- Health, dental, and vision insurance (company covers 90% of premiums for employees, 70% for dependents)
- 401(k) with 4% employer match, vested immediately
- 12 weeks parental leave (birthing and non-birthing parents)
- Mental health support: access to Headspace and 6 counselling sessions per year

## Expense Policy

Business expenses under $200 do not require pre-approval. Expenses between $200 and $1,000 require manager approval. Expenses over $1,000 require VP or CEO approval. All expenses must be submitted within 30 days via the Expensify integration in Slack. Receipts are required for all expenses over $25.

## Code of Conduct

We are committed to a harassment-free workplace. All employees must complete annual diversity and inclusion training. Violations of the code of conduct should be reported to Rachel Kim (Head of Ops/HR) or through the anonymous reporting form linked in #general. Reports are investigated within 5 business days, and confidentiality is maintained throughout the process.
